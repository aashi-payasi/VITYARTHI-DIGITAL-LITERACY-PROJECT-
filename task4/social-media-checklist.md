“This checklist promotes responsible digital citizenship and safe online behavior.” 



“Your digital footprint is your online reputation—protect it wisely.”

Do's
Do think carefully before posting anything online; remember that posts can stay permanently.
Do maintain a respectful and professional tone while interacting with others.
Do use strong passwords and enable two-factor authentication for account security.
Do adjust privacy settings to control who can view your profile and posts.
Do verify news and information before sharing to prevent spreading misinformation.
Do build a positive digital footprint that reflects your personality and achievements.
Do respect others' opinions, cultures, and beliefs while communicating online.
Do use social media for learning, networking, and career opportunities.
Do report inappropriate or harmful content when necessary.
Do take breaks from social media to maintain mental well-being.
Don'ts
Don't post offensive, abusive, or inappropriate content that may harm your reputation.
Don't share personal or sensitive information like address, phone number, or passwords.
Don't engage in cyberbullying, trolling, or online fights.
Don't accept friend or follow requests from unknown or suspicious accounts.
Don't spread fake news, rumors, or unverified information.
Don't overshare your personal life or location publicly.
Don't use social media during important academic activities or classes.
Don't click on suspicious links or messages (may lead to scams or hacking).
Don't compare your life constantly with others; avoid negative impact on mental health.
Don't ignore digital etiquette and responsible online behavior.
