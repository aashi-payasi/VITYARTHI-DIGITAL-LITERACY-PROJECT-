                                       ## **RANSOMWARE** ## 

**Ransomware is a type of cybercrime in which attackers block access to a computer system or encrypt important files and demand money (ransom) to restore access.**
## How Ransomware Attacks Happen
Ransomware attacks exploit human error and weak security, making awareness and prevention the strongest defense.


# STEP1 : Target Identification
Cybercriminals identify vulnerable users such as students, small businesses, or organizations with weak security systems.
# STEP2 : Initial Infection (Delivery Stage)
The ransomware is delivered through deceptive methods like phishing emails, fake websites, malicious ads, or infected software downloads.
# STEP3 : User Interaction (Activation)
The attack begins when the user clicks on a harmful link, opens an infected attachment, or installs untrusted software.
# STEP4 : Silent Installation
The ransomware is installed on the device without the user’s knowledge and may disable security systems.
# STEP5 : Execution and Spread
The malware runs in the background, scanning files and sometimes spreading to other connected devices or networks.
# STEP6 :  Data Encryption
Important files are encrypted using advanced algorithms, making them completely inaccessible to the user.
# STEP7 : Ransom Demand Display
A message appears demanding payment (usually in cryptocurrency) in exchange for a decryption key.
# STEP8 : Pressure and Threats
Attackers impose deadlines and may threaten permanent data loss or data leaks to force quick payment.

## WHO ARE USUALLY TARGETED 
Ransomware attackers mainly target users who have valuable data but weak security or low awareness.

# College Students
Students are easy targets because they often download free software, click unknown links, and may not use strong security measures.
# Small Businesses & Startups
They usually lack advanced cybersecurity systems, making them more vulnerable to attacks.
# Hospitals & Healthcare Systems
These organizations depend on urgent access to data, so attackers target them knowing they are more likely to pay quickly.
# Educational Institutions
Universities and colleges store large amounts of data but often have weak network security.
# Remote Workers
People working from home may use unsecured Wi-Fi networks and personal devices, increasing risk.
# Non-technical Users / General Public
Individuals who are less aware of cyber threats are more likely to fall for phishing scams.


## what the consequences are??
Ransomware can cause financial loss, data damage, and serious disruption to personal and professional life.
# Data Loss or Inaccessibility
Important files such as documents, projects, and personal data become locked or permanently lost.
# Financial Loss
Victims may have to pay ransom money, and even then, there is no guarantee of data recovery.
# Disruption of Work/Studies
Students may lose assignments, and organizations may face shutdown of operations, causing delays and missed deadlines.
# Emotional Stress and Panic
Losing valuable data can cause anxiety, fear, and frustration, especially when deadlines are near.
# Damage to Reputation
Businesses or individuals may lose trust and credibility if sensitive data is compromised.
# Data Breach Risk
Attackers may leak or misuse personal or confidential information.
# Recovery Costs
Expenses for repairing systems, hiring experts, and restoring data can be high.

A well-known real incident is the AIIMS Delhi ransomware attack, which affected one of India’s largest hospitals. In this attack, hackers gained access to the hospital’s systems, possibly through a compromised network or phishing method. Once inside, the ransomware spread across servers and encrypted critical patient records and hospital data.

The attack disrupted hospital services for several days. Doctors and staff were forced to switch to manual record-keeping, causing delays in patient care and administrative work. Reports suggested that attackers demanded a large ransom in cryptocurrency to restore the data, although authorities worked to recover systems without paying.
