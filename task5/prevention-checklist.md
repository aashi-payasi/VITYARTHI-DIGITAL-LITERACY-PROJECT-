**PREVENTION CHECKLIST FOR COLLEGE STUDENTS:**
Following these safety measures helps students protect their digital identity, financial assets, and personal data from cyber threats.

##  Prevention Tips

- Use strong and unique passwords for each account (mix of letters, numbers, and symbols).
- Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on email, social media, and banking apps.
- Never share OTPs, passwords, or bank details with anyone—even if they claim to be from the bank.
- Avoid using public Wi-Fi for logging into sensitive accounts like banking or email.
- Always verify links before clicking; avoid suspicious messages, emails, or unknown attachments.
- Keep your devices updated with the latest security patches and antivirus software.
- Limit the personal information you share on social media (location, phone number, etc.).
- Download apps only from trusted sources like Google Play Store or Apple App Store.
- Log out from devices after use, especially on shared or public computers.

##  UPI & Financial Safety Tips

- Double-check UPI IDs and QR codes before making any payment.
- Never approve "collect requests" (payment requests) from unknown people.
- Do not scan random QR codes to receive money—this is a common scam.
- Enable transaction alerts (SMS/email) for all bank and UPI activities.
- Immediately block your bank account or UPI if you notice suspicious activity.

##  Reporting Cyber Crime

- Report cyber fraud at the **National Cyber Crime Portal**: https://www.cybercrime.gov.in
- Call the **Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930** for immediate assistance in financial fraud cases.
